Help & Information About Bankruptcy Prediction

Understanding Bankruptcy

Bankruptcy is a legal process through which companies unable to meet their debt obligations seek protection or reorganization. Early detection of financial distress helps prevent losses to investors, employees, and creditors.

FinShield helps businesses and analysts by providing early bankruptcy risk warnings based on financial data.

About the FinShield Model

FinShield uses advanced machine learning, specifically an XGBoost classifier, to predict bankruptcy risk based on historical financial data. Key financial ratios and indicators are used as input features:

  • Profitability Ratios: Such as return on assets (ROA), return on equity (ROE).
  • Liquidity Ratios: Current ratio, quick ratio to assess the company’s ability to pay short-term debts.
  • Leverage Ratios: Debt-to-equity ratio, which shows financial risk due to borrowing.
  • Cash Flow Metrics: Operating cash flow trends that reflect financial health.

The model was trained on a large dataset of company financials with known bankruptcy outcomes and validated for over 90% accuracy, making it a reliable tool for risk prediction.

How to Use FinShield

To get started with FinShield:

  • Upload your company’s financial statements including income statements, balance sheets, and cash flow reports.
  • FinShield processes the data and provides a risk score indicating the likelihood of bankruptcy.
  • Download detailed reports to review the insights or share with stakeholders.

Our goal is to empower you with data-driven insights so you can take proactive financial decisions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What financial data formats are accepted?

We accept Excel (.xlsx), CSV files, and some PDF formats containing structured financial statements.

Is my financial data kept confidential?

Yes. All data is encrypted during upload and processing, and we never store your data without explicit permission.

How often should I run the bankruptcy check?

We recommend quarterly checks or whenever there are significant changes in your company’s financial status.

Can I customize the risk thresholds?

Currently, FinShield uses default risk thresholds based on industry standards, but we plan to introduce customizable settings soon.
